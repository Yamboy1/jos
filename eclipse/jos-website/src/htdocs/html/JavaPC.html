Update 16 April 98:

JDC memebers can download beta 2 of JPC It boots from dos into an austere windowing system and you can run HotJava (HJ) or jdk 1.1.4 programs, although only HJ is included. Seems to have all sorts of support for network tricks like DHCP and NIS and such, which I've no way to play with. The beta will expire in 60 days after download. Without HotJavaViews or eSuite or something, I don't see how it could be any use to anyone who isn't a developer...but it runs reasonably well on a 486 with 16 megs in MSDOS 7. Maybe when the new beta of FreeBuilder is released, the combination will prove interesting...

Previously:

Sun has proposed a product called JavaPC that will deliver a JVM that runs along side Dos and Windows with full JDK compatibility. The product is due to ship Q298 at a price of about 100 US dollars.

See: http://java.sun.com/products/javapc/index.html
