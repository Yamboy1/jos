URI?

  • Just a quick question: what does "URI" stand for anyway?
    http://jos.org/pipermail/arch/1999-August/000107.html
  • It stands for "Universal Resource Indicator."
    http://jos.org/pipermail/arch/1999-August/000108.html
  • Actually it means "Uniform Ressource Identifier".
    http://jos.org/pipermail/arch/1999-August/000109.html
  • to be clear, a URL is a type of URI (not vice-a-versa)
    http://jos.org/pipermail/arch/1999-August/000204.html
  • There are URLs (which you're familiar with), and URNs (Uniform Resource Name) which are like URLs but without a machine name. http://jos.org/pipermail/arch/1999-August/000129.html
  • I thought it was the other way around... http://jos.org/pipermail/arch/1999-August/000133.html
  • 'N' does stand for Name. But 'R' also stands for Resource.
    http://jos.org/pipermail/arch/1999-August/000137.html
