SkillPages

Article contributed by GilbertHerschberger (6 December 2001).

Introduction

Thankfully, the Imported forum is rendered for backward compatibility and read-only, unlike than other forums on JOS Wiki. Over time, articles should be reworked and saved in on of the other forums.

Procedure

The following procedure illustrates how to rework an Imported article.
  • Choose an article that has no unsupported features. An imported article may use HTML tags directly. Some of these tags cannot yet be reproduced by the sfWiki software.
  • Edit the article on-line.
  • Enter a descriptive summary of the article. All of the Imported articles have a non-descript "Imported from another Wiki" summary.
  • Select an appropriate forum for this article. Most articles go in the Main forum. User articles go in the User forum.
  • While editing, remove unsupported HTML tags. Here are a few hints:
    • Rework all links. The anchor tag is not supported, either as a name or href. Enclose the link in parentheses.
    • Remove the horizontal-line tag (<HR>). Replace it with four hyphens.
    • Remove the begin-paragraph tag (<P>). Remove the end-paragraph tag (</P>). Be careful to add a blank link to the wiki text to create a needed end of paragraph.
    • Remove the begin-unordered-list tag (<UL>). Replace each list-item tag (<LI>) with 3, 6 or 9 spaces and an asterisk (*). Remove blank lines within the list item because a blank line automatically ends the list item. Remove the end-unordered-list tag (</UL>).
    • Remove the begin-ordered-list tag (<OL>). Replace each list-item tag (<LI>) with 3, 6 or 9 spaces and a hash mark (#). Also remove blank lines within the list item. Remove the end-ordered-list tag (</OL>).
  • Conserve original formatting as much as possible. Sometimes, formatting of imported articles cannot be preserved. Your good judgement is welcomed.
  • Conserve the start-of-code and end-of-code, the start-of-preformat and end-of-preformat tags. In some preformatted text, it may be necessary to manually restore three spaces which have been inadvertently replaced by a tab, six spaces which have been inadvertently replaced by two tabs.
  • Preview the page after making your changes. Words in all capital letters may be linked unexpectedly. Links to pages which do not yet exist may now clutter the article. The nolink tag should be used to remove links which do not make sense.
