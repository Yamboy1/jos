Introduction

What kind of project is this? Is it a software project? Is it a research project? Maybe we can use the purpose of a research project to figure it out.

Definition

Let's begin by defining a research project. A project produces something. Something produced by a project is always a product. A project produces a tangible product people can use.

A research product also produces a tangible product people can use. It produces information, the biproduct of research. Other people are able to skip the time-consuming process required to reach conclusions by gathering the information produced by other research projects.

Publication is critical to the success of a research project. A research project places emphasis on mailing lists and publication of research articles. Publication provides common ground. It also increases efficiency by eliminating some duplication of effort. Each researcher publishes what they have discovered.

About the JOS Project

I believe the JOS Project is a successful research project. Mailing lists and JOS Wiki are useful ways to share information, the biproduct of research. Other members of the JOS Project are able to skip over the time-consuming process of understanding the implications of multiple bytecode process by reading open discussions on the mailing list and many articles on JOS Wiki.

Publication is critical to the success of the JOS Project. Without the mailing lists and JOS Wiki, there might be no forum for the exchange of ideas related to a free Java-based operating system. The purpose of the JOS Project may not be to develop operating system software, but help others develop it.

Purpose

The purpose of the JOS Project may change from time to time. While the original purpose may have been the construction of a single kernel/virtual machine, this has never been successful. Everyone cannot possibly agree to the best way to build a Java-based operating system. We don't want everyone to agree on implementation details. Our goal was to make everyone aware that a Java-based operating system is possible. I think we have done that.

Success came when JJOS was started as a subproject. Messages on the mailing lists are products. The articles on JOS Wiki are products. They are the tangible evidence that research has been performed by other members.

The JOS Project won't be the first to market. Other projects have already produced hardware/software to build a Java-based operating system. Some of them are open source and free license.

Members: If your JOS-related research is not reflected on the JOS Wiki, other members must go through the time-consuming process of doing their own research and coming to their own conclusions.

Subprojects

As a rule, subprojects produce software. The JOS Project itself brings together many subprojects under a common umbrella. Also, the JOS Project serves as an incubator to spawn new and exciting subprojects.

Because of the massive size of the JOS Project, little but research can be accomplished at the project level. The project might never produce a single piece of software that every member will enthusiastically endorse. The JOS Project represents part of the operating system industry. It has many competing members.

The JOS Project enables competing members to maintain a friendly competition. Each member contributes to the knowledge of the industry as the specialize in their own product or product line.

Summary

As a research project, the JOS Project has been very successful. As far as producing software that people can use, it has been disappointing. On the other hand, subprojects have been very successful in producing software.
