AboutJOSTechnicalEdition; DownloadJOS

24 April 2001

  • The "old" JOS Wiki at www.metamech.com has been locked.
  • Version 1j is the latest release of the JOS Technical Edition.
  • See also DownloadJOS, DownloadStaticWiki, MiniServer
  • The XBrowser software has been added to the distribution.
-- GilbertHerschberger

23 April 2001

  • Since JOS Wiki at www.metamech.com has been locked, it was necessary to create and update articles for Static Wiki directly in HTML. The JOSDistribution1j and NewArticles1j were created. DownloadJOS, DownloadStaticWiki and glossary articles A thru Z were updated. Corresponding articles on the JOS Wiki at wiki.jos.org are needed.
  • The NewArticles1j article contains a list of new articles for this version. Corresponding changes have been made to the glossary (TheGlossary) for Static Wiki. Changes need to be made to the new JOS Wiki at wiki.jos.org.
  • The limited on-line version of the JOS Technical Edition at http://www.jos.org/redist/ has been updated.
  • Added the XBrowser product to this distribution.
  • Modified the StaticWikiProgram to link off-line articles to the "new" sfWiki software at wiki.jos.org.
  • Modified the StaticWikiProgram to remove the following message from an off-line article: "This Wiki is locked! The current active Wiki for the JOS project has moved to the new sfWiki hosted site at sourceforge.net. Go to http://wiki.jos.org and register for a new WikiName/password and get wiki'ing on the new and improved system!. Comments, questions, etc should be sent to the jos-general@lists.sourceforge.net mailing list. This wiki will be locked for a few days then I'm going to turn all links to auto-redirects to the wiki.jos.org site."
-- GilbertHerschberger

21 April 2001

A snapshot of many articles on the old JOS Wiki at www.metamech.com was taken yesterday. A snapshot of a handful of articles was taken this morning. These articles have been converted into static HTML pages and will be available soon as a new release of Static Wiki. -- GilbertHerschberger

19 April 2001

The next version of the JOS Technical Edition is being planned. It will be created during the month of April 2001. This version continues where version 1i left off, making it easier to find out more about a software product. -- GilbertHerschberger
