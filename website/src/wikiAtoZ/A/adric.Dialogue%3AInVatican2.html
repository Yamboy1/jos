The documents published by the Second Vatican Council represent great leaps of progress for the Roman Catholic Church in its relations to other Christians and to other religions. Two of the ?? documents in particular directly address this new benevelonce? to other faiths. Unitatis Redintegratio positions the Catholic Church as (interested) in reconcilliation and eventual reunion with other Christians, both Orthodox (or Eastern) and Protestant. Nostra Aetate? explores the common grounds of Christianity, Islam, Judiasm, and Hinduism (sort?) and expresses hope for .. (amongst them).

U R is subtitled, in the English translation, as Decree on Ecumenism. It describes an ecumenical movement in which individuals and organizations move towards unity for all Christians. In particular it relates the official Catholic doctrine on ecumenism, constructing it from the accounts of the early development of Christianity found in the Epistles of the New Testament of the Bible, and describing the regretable divisions that began shortly thereafter and continued for many centuries. Against these historical trends are presented the intructions of Jesus Christ and his apostles about the founding and conduct of the Church. The different derivations of Protestant and Eastern (Orthodox) churches are handled separately. From this (conflict) the council then contructs guidlines on the practice of ecumenism. Catholics are encouraged to pray for unity, even together with "their separated brethren"UR 8, but are cautioned about actually worshipping with them. The decision about the appropriateness of this (sort) of outreach is left to the local churches and their bureaucracy. Theological debate with (..) is recommended both to strengthen Catholic's understanding of their own beliefs and to better understand the point of view of others. UR 9 (Theologians) are warned that both belief and practice should not further alienate other sects, but that Catholic doctrine must be (still) presented in it's entirety (as well). The decree closes by urging all Catholics to work together with other Christians towards unity, recognizing that this goal cannot be achieved without divine intervention.

Nostra Aetate is a much more succinct? document. It dwells less on history or scripture as it rapidly breaks down the (divisions) amongst all religions, from a Christian standpoint. (While) It finds commonality with other world religions (specifically Hinduism, Buddhism, Islam, and Judiasm) and Christianity by examining the beliefs they have in common, in particular the common myth and ancestry of the Abrahamic religions. Concealed in the revolutionary rhetoric of this short declaration is a passage which absolves all Jews of guilt in Christ's death. This had been a major point of anti-Semitic movements throughout Christian history, and it is in this decree that the Catholic Church reverses its position completely, and prohibits anti-Semitism. In fact, it expresses hope that when the Messiah returns, at the end of this world, the Jewish people will recognize him and be united with "all peoples". NAe 4 This is demonstrative of the role these documents have in the movement towards ecumenical dialogue with other faiths. Past wrongs are regretted and forgiven equally, and all are urged to move towards peace and unity. No action is prescribed by these documents, even as they fundamentally alter the role of the Catholic Church with respect to other religions.
