IndexPages

Article contributed by GilbertHerschberger (25 January 2000, revised November 20, 2000).

Introduction

Articles are often grouped together and listed in a CustomPages article. When closely-related articles are grouped together this way, it enables readers to enjoy many articles contributed on a specific topic. See also IndexPages for many examples of index pages.

Purpose

An index page is multi-purpose.
  • An index page enables a reader to see the material as an author sees it. Some index pages are organized chronologically, with the oldest articles at the top. Some index pages are organized spacially, with the most important articles are at the top.
  • By reviewing all articles on a specific topic, it might inspire an author to contribute an article that has not yet been written. The JOS Wiki remains a inspiring place of opportunity for members to express their views.

Automation

No doubt the JOS Wiki would benefit from automation. It needs an automatic glossary, providing the full list of all articles in alphabetical order. It needs an automatic changes page, providing a full list of all articles in the order they were contributed. It needs a full text search, providing a list of all articles matching an artibrary phrase or regular expression. And yet, this is not enough.

Automation cannot replace index pages. Index pages are an important part of the tour. Providing a table of contents may not enable a reader to see the material as the author sees it. A full text search may not enable a reader to find the most important articles first. It is most useful to have stewards arrange articles into tours. A reader with limited resources should always read the most important articles first.

Recommended reading

There two kinds of recommended reading on the JOS Wiki. The first kind provides comprehensive links to closely-related articles on the Internet. The second kind provides links to closely-related articles on JOS Wiki. Both provide a way for members to recommend articles to other members, and both are needed.
