The law of jubilee is given in the 25th chapter of Leviticus. The tenth verse summarizes the specific instructions which take up the remainder of the chapter: Consecrate the fiftieth year and proclaim liberty throughout the land to all its inhabitants. It shall be a jubilee for you; each one of you is to return to his family property and each to his own clan. --The Student Bible (NIV)NIV

Generally jubilee were decreed by an interval of years, although the interval was altered repeatedly over the centuries, and several time interupted? by war. In November 1994 Pope John Paul II officially declared the Great Jubilee of 2000 with the Apostolic Letter Tertio Millennio adveniente. This document briefly gives the history of the jubilee, and it's theological background. The importance of this jubilee is explored, both historically and spiritually. It also provides for the creation of a committee and other preparations to take place in the three years leading up to 2000. The previous declarations of jubilee often had some cause at that core. Often beatifications and canonizations take place on such a date.
