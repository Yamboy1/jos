Introduction

This product enables you to run a private wiki at home on your own equipment or run a corporate wiki on your organization's intranet. Of course, it also enables you to run your own public wiki on the web.

Book

The QuickiWiki product is distributed with The Wiki Way, a book available at your local bookstore. If you're planning to build your own wiki-compatible software, this book can serve as your complete wiki specification. It elaborates on the way your wiki should work and the specific wiki tags it should support.
  • http://www.wiki.org/
  • Honorable Mention: Our own JOS Wiki was given an honorable mention in this book.

Markup

The markup for QuickiWiki and sfWiki are different. QuickiWiki uses the exclamation mark (!) to support headings.
  • Two exclamation marks indicates heading 2.
    • This is equivalent to sfWiki's <H2>sometext</H2>.
  • Three exclamation marks indicate heading 3.
    • This is equivalent to sfWiki's <H3>sometext</H3>.
  • Four exclamation marks indicate heading 4.
    • This is equivalent to sfWiki's <H4>sometext</H4>.
Like sfWiki, QuickiWiki uses an asterisk (*) to support unordered lists, but does it in a different way.
  • One asterisk ('*sometext') indicates level 1.
    • This is equivalent to sfWiki's '[tab]*'
    • or its less recommended cousin: '[three spaces]*'.
  • Two asterisks ('**sometext') indicate level 2.
    • This is equivalent to sfWiki's '[tab][tab]*'
    • or its less recommended cousin: '[six spaces]*'.
Unlike sfWiki, QuickiWiki uses pairs of multiple single quote (') characters to support emphasized and strong text. This is far more likely to give correct results than either the more frequently used underscore (_) or asterisk (*).
  • Two single quotes (''sometext'') indicate emphasized text.
    • This is equivalent to sfWiki's '[underscore]sometext[underscore]'.
  • Three single quotes ('''sometext''') indicate strong text.
    • This is equivalent to sfWiki's '[asterisk]sometext[asterisk]'.

Wordwrap

Like the old JOS Wiki, QuickiWiki includes WORDWRAP="VIRTUAL" option when displaying a text area, so that text is wrapped within the width of the text area.
