I agree with Ian. Since you got a working cvs on sourceforge,
and since the cvs on jos.org is probably way too flakey to be
worth trying to fix it, the only reasonable solution is to move
it's content to the working cvs.

Any scripts that are a potential embarrassment should not be
on a multi client server. You can't expect dozens of people who
each PAY for 200 to 500 GIG traffic and high reliability to just
ignore it when ONE non-commercial client denies service to
everybody else with flakey and sloppy code.

And that is exactly what happened yesterday.
/sites/jos/www/cvsweb.cgi  performed a Denial Of Service
attack from the inside out and denied service to many
thousands of visitors to the sites of over a hundred clients.

NOBODY can be allowed to do that on a multi-client server.
Scripts like that belong on home machines, not public servers.

Jos.org should carry the public information and be the central
index or gateway to the different home machines that use
potentially embarrassing scripts.

Helmut
